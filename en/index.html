『Investment ✕ Case Study ✕ Global』
Meet the new normal of Taiwan startups from International VC perspectives！

The continuous trend of digitalization and rapid innovative changes are the new normal of the current industry. Innovation has become the essential drive to economic development, and it has prompted enterprises to seek breakthroughs for growth. The skyrocketing of entrepreneurship and the downturn with the pandemic proved that this is the best timing to build startups. 

2021 G Camp: Foresee the Future Stars and Co-Create New Dreams" invites international and local venture capitals, and previous startup teams to participate, and share their observations to enhance market sensitivity, and to optimize products and business models with the new startups. Furthermore, G Camp Project provides a comprehensive test field, plentiful resources, and international connections, to help startups to reshape their business models and expand potential business opportunities. 

Global VC
Direct Insights

We invite international VCs to share their observations and the next opportunity for Taiwan’s startups, and the key factors to get funded. We also invite local VCs to comment and give feedback on the Startup Demo Pitch.

Massive Resource
Heading to the World

Meet Taipei goes to Southern Taiwan for the first time! Across the north and the south, Taiwan's startups can realize their dreams to become international startups through our plentiful resources!

Startup Training
Result Feedback

To solve the pain points of startups, we invite senior G Camp startups to share their experience and takeaways with the project.

Speakers

Venture Capital

Jeffrey Paine

Golden Gate Ventures, Founding Partner

Jeffrey Paine is a Co-founder and Managing Partner of Golden Gate Ventures, an early stage technology venture capital fund based in Singapore investing in Southeast Asia with over US$175 million under management investing in over 45 companies since 2012. Jeff started and manages the Founder Institute in Singapore where he is currently overseeing its expansion in Southeast Asia, and Japan. Since 2010 the Founder Institute in Singapore has graduated over 100 companies. He is a recipient of the Founder Institute Director Award 2012 for “Greatest Ecosystem Impact” Worldwide (Singapore). He is a Singapore native and graduated with a Bachelors of Business Administration (Information Systems) from the University of Southern California in Los Angeles.

JUDGE

Jason Lu

Director of Darwin Venture

Jason joined Darwin Venture in January, 2020. From 2012 to 2019, Jason served as the manager of Garage+ incubator and liaison office of Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) at Epoch Foundation. He was responsible of portfolio management, investor and corporate partnership. He had supported more than 350+ start-ups, including 150 international start-ups, in AI, BigData, IoT, Digital Health, and lifestyle fields. The average funding rate reach to 75%. Prior to Garage+, Jason used to work at YFY Group (Yuen Foong Yu) for new business development from 2005 to 2007. Jason also joined 3 start-ups in mobile and telecom industries between 2007 and 2010. He also served as advisor and mentor in several universities, companies, and government projects in recent years.

Dien Changan

Partner of Deloitte Taiw

Dien joined Deloitte in 2004 starting in the audit department for financial industry in Deloitte Taipei office. After 2 years, Dien transferred to Hsinchu Science-Based Industrial Park in 2006 audit for high-tech companies such as TSMC. After two years of strong experience related to the high-tech and manufacturing companies, Dien was selected by Deloitte Taiwan firm to be the only person in exchange program to Deloitte San Jose office for 18 months. Dien was an Audit Senior in-charge of an PCAOB engagement of software company in San Jose office. After exchange program and back to Taiwan, Dien was promoted to Manager in 2010 and services Taiwan listed companies, U.S. Foreign Private Issuers (TSMC and SMTC) and also Foreign Issuers in Taiwan stock markets. In 2012, Dien transferred to Taipei to serve most of the bio-tech companies, financial bank, financial holdings, and startup companies and promoted to Senior Manager in 2013. Dien is currently Audit Partner in Deloitte and serve Chunghwa Telecom group and other Taiwan listed companies. Dien has been providing service to companies in Taiwan, China, U.S., Singapore and Malaysia. Dien is familiar with R.O.C. GAAP, U.S. GAAP and also IFRSs. The clients are including telecom, semiconductor, IC designs, bio-tech, financial holdings, healthcare, and manufacturing companies. Dien is well experienced in the Initial Public Offering process related to Taiwan and U.S. Markets.

Flora Hsieh

Program Director of SparkLabs Kaohsiung Co., Ltd.

Flora Hsieh is the program director of SparkLabs Taipei, a Venture Capital with Accelerator arm to help local entrepreneurs to go global. The mentorship-driven program targets startups in Artificial Intelligence, VR/AR, mobile application, fintech, e-commerce, digital healthcare, cybersecurity, industrial automation, and other verticals. She was previously the Head of Operation of Junyi Academy, an EdTech NPO that provides K-12 students personalized learning tool, and was responsible for fundraising, recruitment and finance. Prior to Junyi Academy, She got the return offer from J.P. Morgan and started her career at J.P. Morgan Corporate Bank, Investor Services, as a client relationship analyst.

Lance Chang

CEO of TWBIO

In 2008, Lance obtained a Ph.D. in the Department of Life Sciences of National Chung Hsing University. He served as a postdoctoral fellow at the Center for Genomics of Academia Sinica and participated in bioenergy and synthetic biology research, and was invited to Keio University in Japan, the University of Chicago, and the US Department of Energy as a visiting scholar to integrate gene editing related technologies and successfully developed PGASO cell editing technology in 2012, which is used in the field of biomass energy and the biosynthesis of natural medicines. Lance founded Trade Wind Biotech in 2016, the very first synthetic biology company in Taiwan. The core of the company is to use probiotic editing technology to change the global supply of natural products. In 2019, he was invited to give a speech at NASA. Trade Wind Biotech has completed the vertically integrated plant In 2020, which will be used to initiate research cooperation and commercial production with many countries. The company is planning to enter the natural astaxanthin and therapeutic probiotics business.
